As one of the stated reasons for the stop, United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald noticed that Carlos Martinez-Romero was traveling at a very low rate of speed when United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald first passed him. United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald testified that Carlos Martinez-Romero traveled approximately fifty miles per hour on Interstate 35 where the stated speed limit was seventy miles per hour. Additionally, United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald observed that Carlos Martinez-Romero appeared very tense and rigid. When United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald turned his vehicle around to follow Carlos Martinez-Romero, he testified that Carlos Martinez-Romero consistently looked in Carlos Martinez-Romero's rear-view mirror and weaved within his lane. Further, United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald testified that as he pulled alongside the crew-cab truck used by Carlos Martinez-Romero, Carlos Martinez-Romero moved away from the wheel of the crew-cab truck used by Carlos Martinez-Romero and attempted to position himself between the door jamb of the crew-cab truck used by Carlos Martinez-Romero in between the front door of the crew-cab truck used by Carlos Martinez-Romero and the crew-cab truck used by Carlos Martinez-Romero. While glances in one's rear-view mirror do not necessarily constitute peculiar behavior, we agree that Carlos Martinez-Romero's attempts to hide himself from United States Border Patrol Agent David Fitzgerald's view qualify as a suspicious activity.
