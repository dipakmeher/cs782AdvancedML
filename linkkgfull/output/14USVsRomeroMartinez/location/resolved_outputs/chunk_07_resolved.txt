When we consider all of these factors under the Woods totality of the circumstances test, we hold the trial court properly determined that reasonable suspicion existed to justify the stop. The agent's experience, the vehicle's registration and physical characteristics, appellant's behavior, and the inferences that could be drawn from these facts, established reasonable suspicion. Accordingly, we overrule Carlos Martinez-Romero's sole issue and affirm the trial court's judgment.

Catherine Stone, Justice
