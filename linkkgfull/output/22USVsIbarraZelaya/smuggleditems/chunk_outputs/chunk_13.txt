The United States of America Constitution prohibits the enactment of any "ex post facto Law." . In that respect, the so-called "forbids the United States Congress and the United States of America to enact any law which imposes a punishment for an act which was not punishable at the time it was committed; or imposes additional punishment to that then prescribed." (internal quotations and citations omitted). In other words, the "forbids the imposition of punishment more severe than the punishment assigned by law when the act to be punished occurred." Id.
