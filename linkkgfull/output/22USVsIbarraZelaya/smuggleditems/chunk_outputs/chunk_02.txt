On September 2, 2004, a United States Federal Grand Jury in this district returned the five-count superseding indictment against Jose Marlon Ibarra-Zelaya and the six co-defendants, charging them with the conspiracy to commit hostage taking and assorted offenses stemming from the illegal alien smuggling venture in March 2004, in which the numerous foreign nationals held against their will for ransom were also held against their will for the ransom demand. (Doc. # 108). That superseding indictment charged Jose Marlon Ibarra-Zelaya and the six co-defendants with the conspiracy to conceal, harbor, shield, and transport illegal aliens within the United States of America for commercial advantage and private financial gain in violation of and (counts one, two, and three). The superseding indictment further accused Jose Marlon Ibarra-Zelaya and the six co-defendants of the conspiracy to commit hostage taking by seizing, detaining, and threatening the illegal aliens in order to compel others to pay a sum of money (i.e., the ransom demand) as an explicit and implicit condition for their release in violation of and (count four of the superseding indictment and five).
