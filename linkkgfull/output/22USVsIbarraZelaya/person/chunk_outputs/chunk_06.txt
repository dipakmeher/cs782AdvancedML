To  obtain collateral relief pursuant to ,  a petitioner "must clear a significantly higher hurdle"  than the standard that would exist on direct appeal. .  When a defendant has been convicted and his conviction has been  upheld on direct appeal, there is a presumption that the defendant's  conviction is fair and final. .    "As  a result, review of convictions under ordinarily is limited to questions of constitutional or  jurisdictional magnitude, which may not be raised for the first time  on collateral review without a showing of cause and prejudice." . Of course, this procedural bar does not apply to claims that could  not have been raised on direct appeal, such as those for ineffective  assistance of counsel. See (holding that ineffective-assistance claims are properly raised on  collateral review and not procedurally barred by a failure to raise  them on direct appeal).
