At the rearraignment hearing, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa executed a waiver of the right to plead guilty before a United States District Judge as well as a consent to proceed before a United States Magistrate Judge. (See D.E. 18, p. 1.) In open court, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa agreed to plead guilty to the Indictment, the United States summarized the written plea agreement, United States District Court explained that the United States District Court may consult the United States Sentencing Guidelines in determining his sentence and explained the range of punishment. (Id.) The Magistrate Judge found that Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa had consented orally and in writing to enter the guilty plea before the Magistrate Judge, "fully understands the nature of the charges and penalties," "understands his Constitutional and statutory rights and wishes to waive those rights." (Id., p. 2.) The Magistrate also found that Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa's plea "is made freely and voluntarily," and that he "is competent to enter this plea of guilty," and that "there is an adequate factual basis for this plea." (Id.) The Magistrate Judge recommended that the United States District Court accept the guilty plea and enter final judgment of guilt against Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa. (Id.) In her Report and Recommendation to the United States District Court, the Magistrate Judge noted that "the parties may file objections to this Report and Recommendation within fourteen days after being served with a copy of the Report and Recommendation."
