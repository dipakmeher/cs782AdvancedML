It would have been frivolous to argue that a defendant who had repeatedly illegally returned to the United States after deportation, who had engaged in alien smuggling, who had used aliases, who listed no substantial assets or liabilities on his financial statement was "culturally assimilated" to a degree warranting a downward departure or variance from the indisputably correctly determined guideline range.

Even if counsel had argued more extensively for a downward variance or departure based on Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa's unexceptional family situation and on his "reasons" for knowingly breaking the law by returning illegally after every deportation, the Fifth Circuit has held that a court may consider a defendant's cultural assimilation, but is not required to give it dispositive weight. Thus, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa cannot establish that his counsel was constitutionally deficient on this ground.
