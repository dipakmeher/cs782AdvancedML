During Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa's rearraignment, in a colloquy with the presiding United States District Court for the Southern District of Texas, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa testified that he had time to talk with his counsel and was satisfied with his counsel's help in this case (D.E. 69, p. 6). Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa testified that he understood the rights he was giving up by pleading guilty. (D.E. 43, p. 15-16, 22). Further, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa acknowledged that he understood the consequences of the waiver of appellate rights and his right to "appeal" his conviction and sentence under the provision of. Id. The United States District Court for the Southern District of Texas made the finding that Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa understood the nature of a voluntary plea, and, after Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa entered a plea of guilty, found that his plea was knowing and voluntary and he entered into the Plea Agreement knowingly and voluntarily.
