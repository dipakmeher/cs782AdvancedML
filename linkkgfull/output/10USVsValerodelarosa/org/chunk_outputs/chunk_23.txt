In his third ground for relief, Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa complains counsel was ineffective at sentencing for failing to rely on Amendment 742 of the United States Sentencing Guidelines for calculation of criminal history points. Amendment 742 of the United States Sentencing Guidelines, effective November 1, 2010, eliminated points previously assigned based on the recency of committing the instant offense less than two years after an offense counted in. As noted above, when Rogelio Valero-De La Rosa was sentenced on March 2, 2011, his criminal history calculation included one additional point because the instant offense was committed less than two years after his release from custody. PSR # 32. His criminal history points amounted to 11, placing him in category V. The Amendment 742 of the United States Sentencing Guidelines argument was not advanced at sentencing by counsel.
