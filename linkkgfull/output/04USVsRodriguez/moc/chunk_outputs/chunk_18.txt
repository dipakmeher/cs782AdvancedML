After communication with his supervisor, United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides then 'just told IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. that he needed to go with him to the United States Border Patrol for further questioning, and that he was going to transport him to the United States Border Patrol,' and also advised Freer Police Department Officer Reyes of the same. (May 2nd Hr'g at 3:47 p.m.; May 3rd Hr'g at 1:50, 1:52, 2:42, 3:26 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides stated that he 'didn't give Defendant the option' to leave, but rather 'just told Defendant that 'you're going to come to the United States Border Patrol for further questioning,'' whereby IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. 'agreed to come . . . .' (May 3rd Hr'g at 1:52 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides's and IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.'s testimony made clear that 'United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides didn't ask Defendant for permission or nothing; he just told him' he was going to the United States Border Patrol for questioning. (Id. at 2:42, 3:26 p.m.). It was around this time when United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides also learned that IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.'s vehicle, which is the black Dodge pickup truck, was going to be impounded. (Id. at 2:40 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides then 'kind of escorted IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. to his unit, and Defendant freely went inside' the vehicle without asking any questions; United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides then drove to the United States Border Patrol 20 eighteen miles away and placed Defendant inside the United States Border Patrol, in 'an open area' with other United States Border Patrol agents nearby, before he 'took off' and returned to his assigned area. (Id. at 1:58, 2:42-2:44 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Benavides never informed IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. that he was under arrest and no handcuffs were used. (Id. at 1:46, 1:47, 2:43 p.m.).
