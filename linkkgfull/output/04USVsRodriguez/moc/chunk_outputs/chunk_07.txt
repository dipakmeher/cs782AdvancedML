He noted that it was 'very uncommon' for multiple cars to be turning down Farm-to-Market Road 2050 together, because vehicles on Farm-to-Market Road 2050 are 'mostly ranchers and oil trucks, and if you're going to see a truck in tandem, it's going to be an oil field truck.' United States Border Patrol agent Jorge Luis Trevino acknowledged that there are also 'small cars' on Farm-to-Market Road 2050 due to the presence of a 'wind farm' on the road, but stated that although United States Border Patrol agents 'don't know the people' in those cars, they 'know the vehicles, because they drive every single day to work.' (Id. at 2:16-2:17 p.m.). Those employees often drive 'through the checkpoint, and as soon as they get inspected, they turn around and go back on Farm-to-Market Road 2050.' (May 2nd Hr'g at 2:17 p.m.). When asked what percentage of the vehicles on Farm-to-Market Road 2050 he recognizes, United States Border Patrol agent Jorge Luis Trevino stated that 'ninety, ninety-five percent' of the vehicles are 'farmers and oil field workers.' (Id. at 2:17-2:18 p.m.). Those vehicles that he does not recognize he will 'go out and at least take a look at them.' (Id. at 2:18 P.m.).
