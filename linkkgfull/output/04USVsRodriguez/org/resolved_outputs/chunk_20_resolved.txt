Agent Marco Montemayor described the beginning of the interview as follows: "we, my partner, Marco Montemayor, and I, introduced ourselves, we advised him of his Miranda rights, we then asked him if he understood his rights. After acknowledgment, we asked him if he wanted to speak to us, and by doing so, waive his rights. Then we got him to sign off on the Miranda waiver, and then we began asking . . . about the events that took place and how he subsequently was involved." (Id. at 2:03 p.m.). The law enforcement officers made sure that IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. understood the rights that he was waiving. (May 3rd Hr'g at 2:07, 2:18-2:19 p.m.). After IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. waived his rights and signed a written waiver, he provided the law enforcement officers with a timeline of the previous events, but nonetheless changed a few details of his story several times. (Id. at 2:03, 2:06 p.m.). Agent Marco Montemayor testified that IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. "gave his confession freely and willingly, and . . . was very cooperative" and polite. (Id. at 2:07 p.m.). Significantly, Defendant never stated that he wanted an attorney, nor did he renege on his waiver of his rights. (Id.). Agent Marco Montemayor's testimony also made clear that the law enforcement officers were "very, very specific about their role and their function," never promising leniency or release. (Id. at 2:16, 2:17 p.m.).
