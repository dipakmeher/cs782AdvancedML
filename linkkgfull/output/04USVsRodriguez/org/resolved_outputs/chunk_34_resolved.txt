B. The traffic stop and arrest of IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.

A traffic stop constitutes a seizure under the United States Constitution. Routine traffic stops, being closer in nature to investigatory detentions rather than full blown arrests, are governed by the principles set out in Terry v. Ohio. Pursuant to Terry, the legality of a law enforcement officer investigatory stop is assessed in two parts: (1) whether the law enforcement officer's actions were justified at their inception, and (2) whether the law enforcement officer's subsequent actions were reasonably related in scope to the circumstances that justified the traffic stop.; (en banc). The Supreme Court of the United States has long held that the "touchstone of analysis is reasonableness," (quoting, and "reasonableness, in turn, is measured in objective terms by examining the totality of the circumstances."
