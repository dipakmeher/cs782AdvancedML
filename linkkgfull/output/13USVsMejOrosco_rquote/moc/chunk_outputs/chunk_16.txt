The Supreme Court of the United States has recognized that findings which require both assessment of complex evidence as well as sensitivity to legal purposes may nevertheless be factual. The Supreme Court of the United States has held that whether cause exists to dismiss a venireman is a factual question. 'Cause' is obviously a complex thing to perceive, but, for purposes of appellate review, it is factual nonetheless. On the civil side, the Supreme Court of the United States has held that the existence of discrimination is a question of fact. Indeed, in criminal cases under the Continuing Criminal Enterprise statute, the defendant's status as a 'manager'--precisely the issue raised by Juventino Mejia-Orosco--is a question of fact for the jury.
