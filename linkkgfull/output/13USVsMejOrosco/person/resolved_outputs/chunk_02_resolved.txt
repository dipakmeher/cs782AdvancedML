Juventino Mejia-Orosco drove his van across the bridge into Laredo, Texas. Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales waded the Rio Grande. They did not pass through customs and had no legal right to enter this country. Juventino Mejia-Orosco and Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales then regrouped and spent two days in Laredo. During this time, the group contacted relatives of Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales living in the United States. The family members were instructed to wire Juventino Mejia-Orosco funds. He received $1,000 from Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega's family in Los Angeles and $1,700 from relatives of Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales living in New York City. Two days after arriving, the three drove to the Laredo International Airport. Juventino Mejia-Orosco supplied Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales with airline tickets to San Antonio and the key to a motel room there. Later, Juventino Mejia-Orosco met Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales at the motel and drove them to the San Antonio International Airport. At the airport, Hector Galvencio Paz-Ortega was detained and questioned by immigration officials. He admitted that he and Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales had illegally entered the United States with the aid of Juventino Mejia-Orosco. With this information, law enforcement officials were able to locate and detain Carlos Enrique Cruz-Morales, who was aboard an airplane awaiting departure to New York, and Juventino Mejia-Orosco. A search of Juventino Mejia-Orosco's van uncovered 4,102 United States dollars and 574,000 Mexican pesos.
