After communication with his supervisor, United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides then "just told IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. that he needed to go with him to the Freer Immigration Checkpoint for further questioning, and that he was going to transport him to the Freer Immigration Checkpoint," and also advised Officer Reymundo Reyes of the same. (May 2nd Hr'g at 3:47 p.m.; May 3rd Hr'g at 1:50, 1:52, 2:42, 3:26 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides stated that he "didn't give the defendant the option" to leave, but rather "just told the defendant that 'you're going to come to the Freer Immigration Checkpoint for further questioning,'" whereby IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. "agreed to come . . . ." (May 3rd Hr'g at 1:52 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides's and IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.'s testimony made clear that "United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides didn't ask the defendant for permission or nothing; he just told him" he was going to the Freer Immigration Checkpoint for questioning. (Id. at 2:42, 3:26 p.m.). It was around this time when United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides also learned that IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.'s black Dodge pickup truck was going to be impounded. (Id. at 2:40 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides then "kind of escorted IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. to his unit, and the defendant freely went inside" the vehicle without asking any questions; United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides then drove to the Freer Immigration Checkpoint 18 miles away and placed the defendant inside the checkpoint, in "an open area at Freer Immigration Checkpoint" with other United States Border Patrol agents nearby, before he "took off" and returned to his assigned area. (Id. at 1:58, 2:42-2:44 p.m.). United States Border Patrol Agent Hugo Benavides never informed IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. that he was under arrest and no handcuffs were used. (Id. at 1:46, 1:47, 2:43 p.m.).
