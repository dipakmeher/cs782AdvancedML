B.  The Traffic Stop and Arrest of IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.

A traffic stop on Farm-to-Market Road 2050 constitutes a seizure under the United States Department of Homeland Security.  Routine traffic stops, being closer in nature to investigatory detentions rather than full blown arrests, are governed by the principles set out in the United States Department of Homeland Security.  Pursuant to Terry, the legality of a police investigatory stop is assessed in two parts: (1) whether the officer's actions were justified at their inception, and (2) whether the officer's subsequent actions were reasonably related in scope to the circumstances that justified the traffic stop on Farm-to-Market Road 2050. ; (en banc). The United States Department of Homeland Security has long held that the "touchstone of analysis is reasonableness," (quoting the United States Department of Homeland Security), and "reasonableness, in turn, is measured in objective terms by examining the totality of the circumstances."
