The Supreme Court has repeatedly refused to authorize investigative interrogations at law enforcement stations on less than probable cause or consent. See, e.g., ; . In the present case, IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR.'s involuntary transportation to the Border Patrol station for questioning was "sufficiently like an arrest to invoke the traditional rule that arrests may constitutionally be made only on probable cause." . The pertinent inquiry therefore becomes whether Hugo Benavides had probable cause to arrest IGNACIO RODRIGUEZ, JR. at the time of the traffic stop.
