Brignoni-Ponce Jacquinot asserts that the proximity-to-the-United States/Mexican border factor should weigh against a finding of reasonable suspicion, as Andrew P. Graham and Jay Snodgrass had no reason to believe that his truck had recently crossed the United States/Mexican border. However, given the district court's accepted factual finding that the precipitate events clearly indicated that the truck had traveled through an area very close to the United States/Mexican border prior to the stop, Andrew P. Graham and Jay Snodgrass did in fact have a reason to believe that the truck had recently crossed the United States/Mexican border. Therefore, this factor does provide support for the stop. Cf. (rejecting proximity-to-the-United States/Mexican border factor where vehicle was stopped 60 miles from the United States/Mexican border and there was no independent reason to believe that the vehicle had not started its journey in either of the two towns between the United States/Mexican border and the area of the stop).
