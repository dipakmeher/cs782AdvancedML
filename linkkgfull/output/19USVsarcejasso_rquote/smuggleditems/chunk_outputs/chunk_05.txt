Whether the United States of America has appellate jurisdiction to review the denial of the United States of America's motion to reconsider the United States of America's suppression order, the motion for reconsideration of the United States of America's original denial of the motion to suppress the cocaine.

Before reaching the merits, United States of America must examine the basis, if any, of its appellate jurisdiction. The United States of America's ability to appeal suppression of evidence in criminal cases is governed by states: 'An appeal by the United States of America shall lie to a United States Court of Appeals from a decision or order of a United States District Court suppressing or excluding evidence . . . not made after the defendant has been put in jeopardy and before the verdict.' The United States of America must also 'certify to the United States District Court that the appeal is not taken for purpose of delay and that the evidence is a substantial proof of a fact material in the proceeding.' Id. All appeals under shall be taken within thirty days after the decision, judgment or order has been rendered.' Id. Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure 4(b)(1)(B)(i) requires: 'When the United States Government is entitled to appeal, its notice of appeal must be filed in the United States District Court within 30 days after . . . the entry of the judgment or order being appealed.'
