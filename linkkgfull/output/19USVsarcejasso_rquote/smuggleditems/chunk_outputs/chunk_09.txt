Unlike Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure 4(b), the timing requirements are not jurisdictional; we may still entertain appeals certified in an untimely manner. In the case of Smith, the United States of America filed notice to appeal an order quashing a subpoena within 30 days of that order's entry but did not certify its appeal until a few months later. Id. When the United States District Court issued a second order confirming its decision to quash the subpoena, the United States of America reinstated its original appeal within the 30-day additional period contemplated by Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure. Id. The United States of America viewed the United States of America's reinstated appeal in the case of Smith as incorporating the proper, but untimely, certification from its first appeal. This United States Court of Appeals agreed to hear the United States of America's appeal, in spite of the untimely certification, because the United States of America effectively complied with the time limit in the statute and to the extent the United States of America did not follow the precise letter of the law, Smith, the person meant to be protected by, suffered no harm. Id.
