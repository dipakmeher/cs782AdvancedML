Unlike Federal Rule of Appellate Procedure, the timing requirements are not jurisdictional; we may still entertain appeals certified in an untimely manner. In Smith, United States of America filed notice to appeal an order quashing a subpoena within 30 days of that order's entry but did not certify its appeal until a few months later. Id. When United States of America issued a second order confirming its decision to quash the subpoena, United States of America reinstated its original appeal within 30 days of the second order's entry. Id. United States of America viewed United States of America's reinstated appeal as incorporating the proper, but untimely, certification from its first appeal. United States of America agreed to hear United States of America's appeal, in spite of the untimely certification, because United States of America effectively complied with the time limit in the statute and to the extent United States of America did not follow the precise letter of the law, Smith, the person meant to be protected by, suffered no harm. Id.
