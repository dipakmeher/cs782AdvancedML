The majority correctly notes that this stop by roving United States Border Patrol agents, Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron, requires the application of the multi-factorial test of. Accordingly, we must assess the lawfulness of the stop in light of the totality of the circumstances known to Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron at the time of the stop. A vital element of this test is whether the United States Border Patrol agent who made the stop had "reason to believe that the vehicle in question had come from the United States-Mexico Border." The defendant, Maria De Jesus Garcia, was stopped on an interstate highway in Texas 115 miles north of the United States-Mexico Border. As the majority candidly admits, nothing in this case suggests that Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron had any reason to believe that Maria De Jesus Garcia's Vehicle had come from the United States-Mexico Border.
