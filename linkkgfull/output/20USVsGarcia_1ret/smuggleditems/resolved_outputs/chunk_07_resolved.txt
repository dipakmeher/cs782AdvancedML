Although we find that illegal alien smugglers did not have sufficient information to reasonably suspect that Maria De Jesus Garcia's Vehicle came from the United States-Mexico Border, the Brignoni-Ponce Standard may still be satisfied "if other articulable facts 'reasonably warrant suspicion.'" We are required, however, to examine these facts charily. After a careful review of the record, we note that many of the factors deemed relevant in Brignoni-Ponce Standard and its progeny are present in this case.
