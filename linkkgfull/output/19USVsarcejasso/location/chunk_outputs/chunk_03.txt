On March 19, 2002, a Laredo federal grand jury returned an indictment charging Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso with possession of cocaine with the intent to distribute in violation of and.  Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso pleaded not guilty before a magistrate judge on March 28, 2002. Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso filed a motion to suppress the cocaine on April 9, 2002, arguing his detention at the border checkpoint was unconstitutionally lengthy. After a hearing, the district court denied this motion by written order entered May 23, 2002. A bench trial took place on June 3, 2002. The parties entered a joint stipulation of fact that same day. During the trial, Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso's counsel made clear that Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso agreed with the stipulation "other than the legality of the stop which is the issue that we seek to preserve for appeal by doing this." The United States of America found Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso guilty and entered verdict on June 7, 2002.
