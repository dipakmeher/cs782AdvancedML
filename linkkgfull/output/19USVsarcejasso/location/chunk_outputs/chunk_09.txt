Unlike Rule 4(b), the timing requirements are not jurisdictional; "we may still entertain appeals certified in an untimely manner." In Smith, the United States of America filed notice to appeal an order quashing a subpoena within 30 days of that order's entry but did not certify its appeal until a few months later. Id. When the district court issued a second order confirming its decision to quash the subpoena, the United States of America reinstated its original appeal within 30 days of the second order's entry. Id. This Court viewed the United States of America's "reinstated appeal as incorporating the proper, but untimely, certification from its first appeal." We agreed to hear the United States of America's appeal, in spite of the untimely certification, because the "United States of America effectively complied with the time limit in the statute" and "to the extent the United States of America did not follow the precise letter of the law, the defendant, Smith, the person meant to be protected by, suffered no harm." Id.
