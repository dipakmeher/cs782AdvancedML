Moreover, while it may not always be readily apparent to the parties what evidence is a "substantial proof of a fact material in the proceeding," in Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso's case the evidence considered by the United States of America during the bench trial consisted only of the stipulation of fact. This stipulation detailed the immigration stop and the cocaine discovered as a result of it, and Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso's counsel clearly preserved the issue of the legality of the stop for appeal. A successful appeal on that issue would result in suppression of the cocaine. Thus, the materiality of that very suppression by the United States of America to Raul Rafael Arce-Jasso's ultimate criminal fate under and was entirely clear when the suppression was made and reconsideration denied, even if how the United States of America would later practically deal with that suppression was not. The United States of America did not appeal in time; therefore, we lack jurisdiction to review the suppression decision.
