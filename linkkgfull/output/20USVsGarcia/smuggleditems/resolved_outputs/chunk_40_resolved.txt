Following a lunch break and another approximately ninety minutes of deliberation, the jury in United States v. Garcia at about 2:00 p.m. again informed the United States District Court, in a written note exchanged between the jury in United States v. Garcia and the United States District Court substantially identical to its prior one, that it was deadlocked. The United States District Court then, over objections of Maria De Jesus Garcia's counsel and a motion for mistrial, read the modified Allen charge used in the 5th Circuit Court of Appeals to the jury in United States v. Garcia. At about 4:45 p.m., the jury in United States v. Garcia asked the United States District Court for more clarification of the terms "reasonable doubt" and "common sense." The United States District Court then repeated a portion of his original instructions to the jury in United States v. Garcia. About forty-five minutes later, the jury in United States v. Garcia returned its guilty verdict.
