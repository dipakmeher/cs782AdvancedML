Maria De Jesus Garcia notes that all of the relevant factors must be viewed in light of the specialized experience and training of the marked United States Border Patrol sedan, Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron. To the extent that the experience of these agents, Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron, contributes to the assessment of particular highways as smuggling routes and particular vehicles as common modes of transporting undocumented aliens, it is indeed a factor which we must consider. See United States v. Maria De Jesus Garcia. On the other hand, the subjective knowledge or beliefs of the marked United States Border Patrol sedan agent, Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron, can add nothing to our objective determination of whether the officer, Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron, had reasonable cause to stop the pickup truck camper of Maria De Jesus Garcia, which is the vehicle referred to as the pickup truck camper. As we stated in United States v. Brignoni-Ponce
