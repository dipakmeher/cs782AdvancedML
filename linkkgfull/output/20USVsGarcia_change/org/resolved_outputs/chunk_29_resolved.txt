The United States District Court believed Stanley Pruszenski and Alfred Baron's undisputed testimony on this point, and it is not challenged on appeal. (We observe that in United States v. Pacheco the panel noted that though the testimony of all four aliens implicated Maria De Jesus Garcia, one testified he slept until the marked border patrol sedan stopped, another that he "sat up normally" until it parked off the road, and "none testified that they slouched in the marked border patrol sedan in an attempt to avoid detection.")
